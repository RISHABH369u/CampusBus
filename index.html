CampusBus provides campus bus-tracking and related services (live bus locations, ETA, schedules, optional word game/leaderboard). Below is a clear summary of what we collect, why, retention, and your rights.
1. Overview

CampusBus provides campus bus-tracking and related services (live bus locations, ETA, schedules, and an optional word game/leaderboard). This policy explains what information we collect, why, how long we keep it, and your rights.

2. Data we collect
Account & identity
  • Email & password credentials: Users create an account with an email and a password. We never store plaintext passwords — passwords are stored only in securely hashed & salted form (server-side). Use a strong, unique password.
  • user_id: unique auth identifier for your account.
  • Optional: full_name and phone only if the user provides them (e.g., profile completion) — used to personalize the experience and show leaderboard names.
Location
  • Driver / bus locations: real-time GPS coordinates for buses (via HiveMQ/MQTT). We store only the last known bus location and overwrite it on each update — we do not maintain a historical route archive.
  • Student device location: only collected if the student explicitly opts-in to share their location within the app (for example, to display their own position). Even when opted-in, student location is not stored persistently unless the user explicitly requests persistent storage.
Files / uploads
  • Profile photos or media submitted with feedback/reports (only if uploaded by the user). These help us investigate issues and may be retained per the retention rules below.
Diagnostics & logs
  • Basic device info, IP addresses, timestamps and crash logs to diagnose issues, improve stability, and protect platform security.
Third-party services

We rely on Mapbox (maps/tiles), HiveMQ/MQTT (real-time messaging), and Supabase (authentication & database). These providers process certain data on our behalf to operate the service.

3. Why we collect it
  • Provide live bus locations, ETAs and routing information.
  • Enable account features, leaderboards, and game scores.
  • Receive feedback/reports (with optional photo) to act on issues.
  • Diagnose and fix problems, and protect user safety (diagnostics & logs).
  • Send important service notifications (e.g., app announcements).
4. Sharing & disclosures
  • We do not sell personal data to third parties.
  • We share data with Mapbox, HiveMQ, and Supabase only as needed to provide the service.
  • We will disclose information only when legally required (e.g., court orders) or to protect safety/security.
5. Retention
  • Bus last-location: only the most recent location is stored and overwritten. No historical archive by default.
  • Profile & account info: email, user_id, name, phone are retained while the account exists; removed on account deletion request.
  • Uploaded photos: retained for 90 days by default (or until the user requests deletion).
  • Logs & diagnostics: retained for 30–90 days depending on log type.

(Retention windows are server-configurable and can be adjusted for compliance or legal requests.)

6. Security

All traffic is encrypted via TLS/HTTPS. Passwords are never stored in plaintext — we store only hashed & salted credentials on the server. Server access is restricted, and secrets/keys should be stored server-side (avoid embedding them in app builds). Credentials are rotated periodically as a best practice.

7. Your choices & rights
  • Revoke or grant location permission anytime via your device settings.
  • Request deletion of your account and personal data by contacting rishcrets@gmail.com.
  • Request a copy of your personal data by contacting rishcrets@gmail.com.
8. Children

This app is intended for college/university students and staff. We do not knowingly collect information from children under the applicable legal age (e.g., under 13). If you believe a child’s information was collected, contact rishcrets@gmail.com.

9. Changes

We will post updates to this page and change the “Last updated” date when we make material changes. Significant changes may also be announced in-app.
